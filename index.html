About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Boston Children’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School, working at the intersection of single-cell proteomics, neurobiology, and data science. With dual expertise in wet-lab and computational biology, I translate multi-omics data into clinically actionable insights. My work spans AI, ML, digital health, and translational neuroscience.

Full Biography

My scientific journey has been built on a rare intersection — wet lab experimentation and dry lab computation — a fusion that allows me to not only generate complex biological data, but to truly understand it. I’m Dr. Deeptarup Biswas, currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Neurology, Boston Children’s Hospital | Harvard Medical School, where I develop high-throughput workflows for Single-Cell Omics, integrating mass spectrometry, machine learning, and neurobiology to unlock insights into brain disorders at an unprecedented resolution.

I began at the bench: from culturing cells to running targeted proteomics assays, handling multiple mass spectrometers, and refining sample preparation protocols. These hands-on wet lab skills laid a strong foundation, but I knew the story of biology could not be told by experiments alone. I immersed myself in data science, AI, and big data analytics, building algorithms and pipelines that translated raw omics signals into clinically relevant knowledge.

During my Ph.D. at IIT Bombay, I explored the proteomic signatures of Meningioma Brain Tumors, resulting in high-impact publications, patents, and the Dr. Gargi Vishnoi Memorial Prize for Best Ph.D. Thesis. My passion for translational science deepened through this work, bridging biomolecular insights with potential diagnostics and therapeutic directions.

Throughout my career, I’ve balanced discovery-driven science with real-world healthcare applications. My contributions during the COVID-19 pandemic to national diagnostic research projects earned institutional recognition, and I’ve actively worked on biomarker panels for grade-specific diagnosis of Meningioma, pushing forward the cause of precision medicine in neurology.

I’ve also been fortunate to lead and co-develop innovative digital tools — BrainProt (India’s first brain disease omics database), CoVProt, IBPM, PhosphoMap, Clustome, and others — designed to democratize complex data for researchers, clinicians, and public health experts.

Equally important is my commitment to Digital Health and Education. Under the mentorship of Prof. Sanjeeva Srivastava, I served as a Teaching Assistant at KCDH (Koita Centre for Digital Health), where I helped train and mentor 30+ B.Tech and M.Tech students in applying AI/ML to real-world healthcare problems. From ideation to analysis, I guided student-led projects across proteomics, genomics, wearable tech, and electronic health records, nurturing the next generation of data-driven healthcare innovators.

Whether analyzing a single cell or leading a full-scale digital health project, my goal remains consistent: to advance translational neuroscience by bridging multi-omics data, AI, and clinical knowledge — turning complex biology into meaningful, patient-centered solutions.
